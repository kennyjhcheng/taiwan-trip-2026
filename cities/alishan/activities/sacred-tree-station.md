# Sacred Tree Station 🌳

The station marks the site of the original Giant Sacred Tree — a 2,700-year-old red cypress that fell in 1997. The stump remains, surrounded by towering ancient trees that dwarf everything around them. Cathedral-quiet. One of the most atmospheric spots in Alishan.

## Getting There

Two options from the main Alishan Station area:

1. **Forest Railway (Shenmu Line)** — runs every 15 min from 09:30 to 16:30, takes ~10 min. Covered by park entry (NT$300). Just board — no extra ticket needed for the Shenmu Line within the park.
2. **Walk** — Giant Tree Boardwalk from the main hotel/visitor area, ~40–90 min through ancient cypress. Far more atmospheric than the train. **Recommended.**

## What to See Here

- The sacred cypress stump and the surrounding grove
- Giant Tree Boardwalk (1.4 km elevated walkway)
- Junction to [[cities/alishan/activities/three-generations-trees]] — just a short walk from here
- Junction to [[cities/alishan/activities/sister-ponds]] loop

## Notes

- Included in park admission (NT$300) — no extra charge
- Combined with Three Generations Trees and Sister Ponds, plan ~2–3h total for this area
