# Fenqihu Old Street (奮起湖老街) 🏘️

A charming mountain village at 1,400m, perched mid-way along the Alishan Forest Railway. Famous for its classic railway bento boxes, red-bean-paste doughnuts, and wooden old-street shops. The town sits in a natural hollow (fenqihu = "boiling lake hollow") and has a serene, old-Taiwan atmosphere.

## Getting There from Alishan (without a car)

**By bus:** Chiayi County Bus 7329 runs between Alishan and Fenqihu (~1.5h, ~NT$85). Check schedule at [taiwanbus.tw](https://www.taiwanbus.tw) — limited departures.

**By forest railway:** When the Alishan Forest Railway is operational, Fenqihu is a stop on the main line. Buy a one-way ticket from Alishan Station. Check current operational status as the railway has had sections under repair.

**Half-day trip:** Leave Alishan by 9–10 AM, explore Fenqihu 1–2h, return by early afternoon.

## What to Do

- Walk the short Old Street (10 min end-to-end)
- Try the **famous railway bento box** (鐵路便當) — NT$100–130, the town's signature dish
- Pick up Fenqihu doughnuts (奮起湖甜甜圈) — the other famous snack
- Short walk to the old locomotive display

## Notes

- ⚠️ No car needed but bus schedule is infrequent — plan carefully
- Best combined with an early start from Alishan on Apr 30
- Forest railway availability varies — check [afrch.forest.gov.tw](https://afrch.forest.gov.tw/EN)
