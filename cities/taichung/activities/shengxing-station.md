# Shengxing Station (勝興車站)

A beautifully preserved **Japanese-era wooden railway station** in Sanyi, Miaoli County — the highest point on the old western Taiwan railway line before the new tunnel route was built. The station closed to regular service in 1998 and is now a heritage site. The surrounding mountain village has hiking trails, old tunnels, and the famous **Longteng Broken Bridge** ruins nearby.

## What to Do

- Walk through the station building (free, open daily)
- Hike the old railway tunnel trail through the forest (~1.5 km round-trip)
- Visit nearby **Longteng Broken Bridge (龍騰斷橋)** — ruins of an old stone arch bridge destroyed in the 1935 earthquake, now a haunting landscape monument (~10 min drive away)
- Browse village shops selling local mochi and wood carvings

## Logistics

⚠️ **Most out of the way on this list** — located in Miaoli County, ~1 hour north of central Taichung by car/taxi.

- No practical public transit; requires taxi, rental scooter, or car
- Round-trip taxi from Taichung ~NT$1,200–1,800
- Best as a **half-day standalone trip** — not easily combined with Taichung city activities

## Honest Assessment

If you're short on time, this one's easiest to skip. But if you want a dose of mountain scenery and Japanese colonial history away from the city, it's excellent.
