A compact military veterans' village painted floor-to-ceiling in vivid murals by **Huang Yung-Fu** ("Grandpa Rainbow"), who began painting to save his neighborhood from demolition in 2008. Now a beloved Taichung landmark and one of Taiwan's most photographed spots.

## Tips

- Free to enter; open all day
- Takes **20вЂ“30 min** to walk through fully
- Best **early morning** (before tour buses) or weekday afternoon
- Grandpa Rainbow is sometimes still present вЂ” now in his 90s, he occasionally paints new additions
- Compact site: murals cover every wall, ground, and doorway in the lane
- Small souvenir shop on-site

## Getting Here

- In Nantun District, **south of downtown** вЂ” best reached by taxi (~NT$150вЂ“180 from Central District)
- Can be combined with a trip toward National Theatre / Fengchia (route goes through this area)

## Why It Might Be "Out of the Way"

It's a 15вЂ“20 min taxi ride from most central Taichung activity clusters. Worthwhile if you have a free hour in transit between downtown and Xitun, but adds a detour if you're trying to cover everything in one day.
