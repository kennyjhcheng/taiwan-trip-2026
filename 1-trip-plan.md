**Night:** **Shilin Night Market (士林夜市)** — 🚇 Red Line to Jiantan (~20 min from Ximen) for a first proper night-market baptism. Must-eats: oyster vermicelli (蚵仔麵線), giant fried chicken, stinky tofu, and **ba-wan (肉圓)** (gelatinous meatball stuffed with pork + bamboo shoots in sweet-savory sauce — unique to Taiwan, not to be skipped). Then pack gear and stock supplies for Kaohsiung transfer tomorrow.

### Apr 20 (Mon) — Ride: Kaohsiung → Checheng
**~55 mi (89 km) | ~1,100 ft gain | Moderate**

- Wake up by 6 AM, on bike by 7 AM
- Route hugs southwest coast to Taiwan's southern tip
- Pass **Hengchun Old Town** — intact Qing-dynasty walled city, one of few remaining in Taiwan; worth a brief stop
- Pass Eluanbi (southernmost point of Taiwan)
- Arrive Checheng
- ⚠️ Small town — limited options, confirm accommodation and bike storage
- **Dinner:** Fresh seafood at coastal stalls — you're in a fishing village, grilled catch is the move
- **Night:** Stock up food/water at convenience stores for tomorrow

---

### Apr 21 (Tue) — Ride: Checheng → Taitung
**~74 mi (119 km) | ~4,300 ft gain | Challenging ⚠️**

- Earliest start of the trip — biggest climb day
- Route passes through Shuangliu Forest Recreation Area and Dawu
- Significant mountain climbing — pace yourselves, eat and hydrate constantly
- Descend into Taitung — celebrate, eat well
- **Evening (optional):** **Zhiben Hot Springs** — 15 min south of Taitung city; Atayal-area hot spring soak; medically justified after the hardest ride day (~NT$150–300)
- **Night:** Pack up, stock up

- ⚠️ Small town — limited options, confirm accommodation and bike storage

### Apr 24 (Fri) — Free Day in Hualien → **Taroko Gorge**

**Plan: Day trip to Taroko Gorge** (Kenny's Week 2 plan assumes Taroko is covered today — see `2-trip-plan.md`).

- **Morning (before gorge):** **Qixingtan Beach (七星潭)** — crescent pebble beach with mountain backdrop, 20 min from Hualien city; on the way toward Taroko; stunning in morning light
- Bus from Hualien City to gorge (~40 min, NT$140 day pass)
- **Shakadang Trail** — 3–4h round trip along turquoise marble-walled river
- **Swallow Grotto (燕子口)** — dramatic cliffside walkway
- **Tunnel of Nine Turns** — twisting marble tunnel with river views *(check current access status)*
- Lunch: simple Truku lunchbox at Buluowan or Tianxiang
- Back to Hualien by late afternoon
- Evening: Hualien Night Market — crystal mochi + milk popsicles

⚠️ **Check [taroko.gov.tw](https://www.taroko.gov.tw/en) before visiting** — some trails still closed from 2024 earthquake/typhoon damage.

Alternate ideas for rest of group if they skip Taroko: coastal ride, Hualien city night market, hot springs, or just rest legs.

## 8. Riding Strategy

### Core Rules
- **Wake up early — on bike by 7 AM every riding day.** Starting late = finishing in the dark.
- Taiwan daylight: 6 AM – 6 PM. Do not waste it.
- 7-Elevens and FamilyMarts are every 10–15 km — use them for water, food, and breaks
- Stock up food/water the night before
- Pack everything the night before — minimal morning routine

### Daily Ride Flow

| Time            | Activity                                                |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| 5:30–6:00 AM    | Wake up, eat prepped breakfast, get dressed             |
| 6:00–7:00 AM    | On the bike                                             |
| Midday          | Larger lunch stop (10–15 min). Reapply sunscreen.       |
| Early afternoon | Push through — hardest part mentally                    |
| 3:00–5:00 PM    | Target arrival at destination                           |
| Evening         | Shower, big dinner, rest legs, plan + pack for next day |

### Physical Prep (Do Now)
- Start riding regularly if not already — train your butt to the saddle
- Do back-to-back weekend rides to simulate multi-day fatigue
- Break in your bib shorts before the trip
- Practice clipless pedals if not already confident
- Strong mental game matters as much as fitness
