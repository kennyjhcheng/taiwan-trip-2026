## 2. Destination Guide

### 🏙️ Taipei

**What it is:** Taiwan's capital — ultra-modern, safe, walkable, with world-class food and distinct neighborhoods each with their own personality. After a week of biking through small towns, this hits differently.

**Best experiences:**
- **Beitou Hot Springs** — volcanic sulfur springs 30 min from central Taipei by MRT. Millennium Hot Spring (NT$40–80) is a public outdoor pool. Japanese-era bathhouse museum is free. A slow morning soak after a week of cycling = perfection.
- **Elephant Mountain (象山)** — 20-min hike to a viewing platform with the iconic Taipei 101 skyline panorama. Best at golden hour.
- **Ximending (西門町)** — Taipei's "Harajuku": pedestrian shopping zone with Japanese cultural traces, anime shops, street food, and a lively solo-traveler atmosphere.
- **Yongkang Street (永康街)** — the foodie street. Beef noodle soup specialists, scallion pancakes, Din Tai Fung (the original location), mango shaved ice. Compact, walkable, excellent for solo dining.
- **Maokong (貓空)** — gondola ride up a mountain to traditional teahouses with panoramic city views. Best in the evening. Oolong tea culture experience.
- **National Palace Museum** — one of the world's greatest collections of Chinese imperial art. Worth 2–3 hours, NT$350.
- **Raohe + Ningxia Night Markets** — locals' favorites over the tourist-heavy Shilin. Raohe has the best black pepper buns in Taiwan. Ningxia is beloved for oyster omelette and braised pork rice.

**Time needed:** 1.5–2 days to do it properly. The current plan gives it 1 day (Apr 26) — enough for the highlights but rushed.

**April/May:** Ideal weather (18–23°C). Evenings are lively. Go weekday for night markets to avoid weekend crush.

### ⛩️ Jiufen + Shifen

**What it is:** Two very different northeast Taiwan experiences packed into one day trip from Taipei.

- **Jiufen (九份)** — a former gold-mining town carved into a cliff above the Pacific Ocean. Narrow red-lantern-lit alleyways, teahouses with ocean views, and an atmosphere that supposedly inspired Spirited Away (Miyazaki denies it, but look at it). The **A-Mei Tea House** (阿妹茶樓) is the most photographed building in Taiwan — get a window seat, order tea and taro balls, and absorb the view.
- **Shifen (十分)** — a tiny village on a working railway line. The town's main attraction is releasing sky lanterns over the train tracks between scheduled departures (NT$150–200/lantern). Taiwan's widest waterfall is a 10-min walk from the station.

**Best food:** Taro balls in soup (Jiufen's signature), peanut ice cream rolls, fish balls, various street snacks.

**Time needed:** 1 full day from Taipei (leave 8–9am, return 6–7pm). Works great on a Monday — weekends are extremely crowded.

**Logistics:** Train Taipei → Ruifang → Shifen (1.5h, NT$64 total). Bus Shifen → Jiufen (20 min, NT$25). Bus Jiufen → Taipei direct (1.5h, NT$100). Bring cash.

**Verdict:** Easy to pair with Taipei. Not a difficult add. The question is what *other* destination you pair it with.

### 🏔️ Taroko Gorge (near Hualien)

**What it is:** The world's largest marble canyon — 19km of vertical white and grey cliff walls carved by the Liwu River. One of Taiwan's most iconic natural wonders, and the ancestral home of the Truku indigenous people. The bike route ends in Hualien, right at the gorge's doorstep.

**Best experiences:**
- **Shakadang Trail** — 3–4 hours round trip along a turquoise river, carved through marble walls. Stunning.
- **Swallow Grotto (燕子口)** — dramatic narrow cliffside walkway above the rushing river.
- **Tunnel of Nine Turns** — the gorge's most iconic section: twisting marble tunnel with river views.
- **Buluowan Terrace** — Truku cultural exhibits, wooden boardwalks elevated above the valley, indigenous art and handicrafts.
- **Tianxiang** — mountain village with temples and scenic walks at the gorge's head.

**⚠️ 2026 Note:** Several trails remain closed or partially closed from 2024 earthquake/typhoon damage. **Check [taroko.gov.tw](https://www.taroko.gov.tw/en) before visiting** — the Shakadang and Swallow Grotto sections are typically accessible, but Tianxiang road access has been affected.

**Best food:** Hualien is famous for its mochi (crystal-clear, chewy, unforgettable) and milk popsicles. Taroko village itself has simple Truku lunchboxes. The food highlight is really Hualien City — eat well there before/after the gorge.

**Time needed:** 1 full day based in Hualien. Bus from Hualien City ~40 min (NT$140 day pass).

**Plan:** ✅ Taroko is scheduled for **Apr 24 (Fri) during Week 1's free day in Hualien** — the bike route ends in Hualien Apr 23, so Apr 24 is a natural fit. Day trip from Hualien City (~40 min by bus, NT$140 day pass). See `1-trip-plan.md` Apr 24 entry.

**April:** Perfect weather. Mild temps, lower rainfall. Some trails may be wet but the canyon itself is stunning.

### 🌊 Sun Moon Lake (日月潭)

**What it is:** Taiwan's most beautiful alpine lake — a 33km cycling loop surrounded by tea-covered hills and forested mountains, with deep Thao indigenous culture on its shores.

**Best experiences:**
- **Cycling the lake loop** — 33km, 2–3 hours on an e-bike (e-bikes available NT$400–600/day from Shuishe pier). Passes temples, viewpoints, and mountain scenery. One of the most rewarding easy bike rides in Asia.
- **Wenwu Temple (文武廟)** — dramatic lakeside temple complex with lion statues and sweeping lake views.
- **Ci'en Pagoda (慈恩塔)** — hike up for the best panoramic lake view.
- **Boat tours** — cross the lake to Ita Thao, the Thao indigenous village on the south shore.
- **Sunrise on the lake** — wake early for mist-covered water at the Shuishe pier. Free and spectacular.
- **Formosan Aboriginal Culture Village** — theme park adjacent to the lake covering 9 Taiwanese aboriginal tribes' cultures. Ambitious but can be fun.

**Best food (Thao indigenous):**
- **Bamboo tube rice (竹筒飯)** — sticky rice steamed in bamboo, the Thao people's signature dish
- **President Fish (總統魚)** — famous local carp, grilled or steamed
- **Wild boar sausage** — grilled at lakeside stalls
- **Millet wine** — traditional Thao beverage, herbaceous and mild
- **Thao mochi** — glutinous rice dessert, different from Hualien-style

**Time needed:** 1 night minimum. 2 nights gives you sunrise, a full cycling loop, and time to absorb it. With only 1 night, arrive by early afternoon to make the most of the day.

**Logistics from Taipei:** Bus from Taipei's Zhongxing New Village area or from Taichung HSR station → Sun Moon Lake (~1.5–2h, NT$190–280). From Taichung HSR: Nantou Bus 6670, roughly hourly, ~1.5h.

**April/May:** Ideal season. 18–25°C, clear mountain views, perfect cycling weather.

### 🌲 Alishan (阿里山)

**What it is:** A mist-shrouded mountain at 2,200m covered in ancient red cypress forest, famous for three things: a sunrise above a "sea of clouds," towering trees over 2,000 years old, and a historic narrow-gauge forest railway.

**Best experiences:**
- **Zhushan Sunrise Train (祝山觀日車)** — board at 4:30am for the Zhushan peak viewing station at 2,451m. The sea of clouds fills the valleys below, first light turns peaks gold, and on clear days you can see Jade Mountain (3,952m, Taiwan's highest peak) catching the sunrise. Genuinely one of the most spectacular things you can do in Taiwan.
- **Giant Tree Trail** — elevated boardwalk through 2,000-year-old red cypress. Cathedral-quiet. Humbling scale.
- **Sister Ponds (姊妹潭)** — two linked mountain ponds with misty wooden walkway through the forest.
- **Alishan Forest Railway** — one of the world's great mountain railways, climbing through 5 climate zones in 2.5 hours. Worth riding for the journey itself.
- **Alishan oolong tea** — the region's high-altitude tea is renowned; buy directly from mountain farms.

**Best food:**
- Mountain vegetable stir-fries (wild ferns, bamboo shoots, local greens)
- Sweet potato congee and local mushroom dishes
- Alishan high-mountain oolong tea at mountain teahouses
- Simple breakfast before the sunrise hike

**Time needed:** **2 nights are necessary.** Night 1: arrive, explore forest trails. Night 2: 4am wake-up for sunrise, then morning forest walk, depart afternoon. With only 1 night you'd arrive exhausted and leave before experiencing much.

**Logistics from Taipei:** HSR Taipei → Chiayi (1h, NT$843) + bus Chiayi → Alishan (2h, NT$200). Total ~3.5h. Buy bus tickets in advance — sells out. Alternatively: Alishan Forest Railway from Chiayi (2.5h, NT$400, sit right side going up).

**Logistics from Sun Moon Lake:** Bus from Sun Moon Lake → Chiayi (~2h, NT$230, 2 departures/day at 1pm and 2:30pm only) + bus to Alishan (2h). Total ~4.5h. Doable but a long travel day.

**April/May:** Late April is late cherry blossom season (some remain). May has lighter crowds and lower prices. Sunrise probability: moderate in late April, variable in May. Bring a warm layer — 10–15°C at night.

**⚠️ Book Alishan accommodation early.** Village is small, fills up fast. Aim for 3+ weeks in advance.

### 🏛️ Tainan

**What it is:** Taiwan's oldest city and former capital, with 300 years of layered history and the undisputed title of Taiwan's culinary capital. The birthplace of many dishes that are now iconic across the island.

**Best experiences:**
- **Anping Old Street + Fort Zeelandia** — 17th-century Dutch colonial fort and preserved district
- **Confucius Temple (孔廟)** — Taiwan's first Confucian temple, built 1665; exquisite architecture
- **Temple crawl** — Tainan has 300+ temples; wandering into them is free and endlessly interesting
- **Night markets** — the food specifically is the reason to come here

**Best food (Tainan is THE food city):**
- **Dan zai noodles (擔仔麵)** — Tainan's original contribution: thin noodles in savory shrimp broth, topped with pork and shrimp
- **Coffin bread (棺材板)** — hollowed-out crispy toast filled with creamed curry or stew; Tainan invented this
- **Shrimp rolls, peanut soup, milkfish congee** — unique to Tainan
- **Night market exploration** — historic recipes you won't find in Taipei

**Logistics in Route E:** Tainan slots in neatly **after Alishan**. Bus down to Chiayi (~1.5h) → short HSR hop Chiayi → Tainan (~15 min, NT$140). Then HSR Tainan → Taipei (~1h 45min, NT$1,350) for the final Taipei night on May 3. No backtracking, no dead transit days.

**Time needed:** 2 nights (May 1–2). Day 1: Anping + temples + one major food meal. Day 2: full food day, night market, and a slow morning departure.

---

### 🌿 Other Destinations Worth Knowing

**Wulai (烏來)** — 1.5 hours from Taipei. Atayal indigenous village with a spectacular 80m waterfall, hot springs, and the Old Trail hiking path. Much less touristy than Jiufen. **May is firefly season** at Wulai — a potentially magical evening experience. Easy half-day or full-day add-on from Taipei without major travel.

**Maokong (貓空)** — gondola ride from Taipei up to mountain teahouses with panoramic city views. 2–3 hour evening activity. Beautiful in the dark. Pairs perfectly with a late Taipei dinner.

**Keelung (基隆)** — 40 min from Taipei. Famous Miaokou Night Market is a serious contender for best night market food in Taiwan. Specialty: shrimp meatballs, pot-edge pancake soup, tempura. Easy half-day.

**Taichung** — transit hub between Taipei and the mountains. Rainbow Village (Grandpa Rainbow's hand-painted houses, free), Fengjia Night Market (one of Asia's largest). Worth 3–4 hours if you're passing through.

**Penghu Islands** — offshore island paradise with basalt cliffs, pristine beaches, and world-class snorkeling. **April–June is the best season** (before summer crowds). Requires a 1-hour flight from Songshan. Too much of a time commitment for this itinerary unless you drop 2+ days here and treat it as the main destination.

- May 1 is a long day: 4am Alishan sunrise → bus down → HSR → check in to Tainan by evening. Doable but wake-up is early.
- No time for Penghu Islands, Wulai, or Keelung side trips.
- Skips Taichung (but you pass through on HSR — easy to add a 2-hour Miyahara stop if desired).

**Best if:** You want to see all of Taiwan's big anchors and eat your way through Tainan without feeling rushed. This is the answer to "how do I hit everything without it feeling like a death march?"

## 4. Food Guide: What to Eat and Where

### 🏆 Absolute Must-Eats in Taiwan

**Braised Pork Rice (滷肉飯 — Lu Rou Fan)**
Slow-cooked minced pork belly in soy sauce, rice wine, and spices, served over steamed white rice. Taiwan's national comfort food. Available everywhere — the quality at a good night market stall often beats a restaurant.

**Beef Noodle Soup (牛肉麵 — Niu Rou Mian)**
Considered Taiwan's national dish. Thick wheat noodles in a deeply savory soy-spice broth with tender braised beef chunks. Yongkang Street in Taipei is the epicenter. Order the red braised version (紅燒).

**Oyster Omelet (蚵仔煎 — O A Jian)**
Fresh oysters + egg + sweet potato starch + leafy greens, pan-fried until crispy at the edges with a soft gooey interior, finished with a sweet-savory sauce. A Taiwan original and unavoidable night market experience.

**Gua Bao (刈包)**
Steamed bao bun with thick pork belly, crushed peanuts, coriander, and pickled vegetables. Often called the "Taiwanese hamburger." Every night market has one.

**Black Pepper Buns (胡椒餅)**
Clay-oven-baked buns with intensely seasoned pork and black pepper inside. Raohe Street Night Market's version is legendary.

**Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐)**
Don't let the smell stop you. Deep-fried fermented tofu with a crispy exterior, soft interior. With pickled cabbage and chili. An essential Taiwan experience. Ningxia Night Market has the best version in Taipei.

**Scallion Pancakes (蔥油餅)**
Flaky, buttery layered flatbread loaded with green onions. Eaten plain or with egg. Yongkang Street has the city's best version.

**Bubble Tea (珍珠奶茶)**
Taiwan invented it. Original Chun Shui Tang (春水堂) in Taichung, but there are Taipei locations. Tiger Sugar for brown sugar version. Get the classic milk tea + pearl — avoid the fancy versions.

### 📍 By Destination: Food Highlights

**Taipei Night Markets (ranked by food quality):**
1. **Raohe Street (饒河夜市)** — locals' top pick. Black pepper buns, pork rib soup (排骨湯), grilled squid
2. **Ningxia (寧夏夜市)** — best braised pork rice and oyster omelet in the city
3. **Shilin (士林)** — largest and most famous; good for first-timers but touristy
4. **Tonghua (通化)** — smaller, more local feel, near Da'an

**Taipei Restaurants Worth Seeking Out:**
- **Yongkang Beef Noodle (永康牛肉麵)** — Yongkang Street — famous for decades, queue expected
- **Din Tai Fung (鼎泰豐) — original Xinyi location** — the world-famous soup dumpling institution. Yes it's now everywhere, but this is the original. The XLB here is a reference standard.
- **Fu Hang Dou Jiang (阜杭豆漿)** — lines form at 5am. Best soy milk and savory bing in Taipei. Morning ritual.

**Jiufen:**
- Taro balls in soup (芋圓) from any of the stalls with ocean views — this is the dish
- Peanut ice cream rolls (花生捲冰淇淋)
- A-Mei Tea House (阿妹茶樓) — window seat with tea and taro balls = the classic Jiufen experience

**Sun Moon Lake:**
- Bamboo tube rice (竹筒飯) — Thao tribe staple; buy from vendors near Ita Thao pier
- President Fish (總統魚) — local carp grilled simply at lakeside restaurants
- Wild boar sausage and grilled meats at Ita Thao village stalls
- Sun Moon Lake red tea (日月潭紅茶) — the region's famous black tea variety; buy loose leaf

**Alishan:**
- High-mountain oolong (高山烏龍茶) — buy directly from farms or shops in the village; this is the real product
- Mountain vegetable set meals (山蔬套餐) — bamboo shoots, wild ferns, local greens simply stir-fried
- Sweet potato congee for warmth before the 4am sunrise hike
- Local mushrooms (香菇) — Alishan is a major shiitake growing region

**Hualien (if doing Taroko):**
- Hualien mochi (花蓮麻糬) — crystal-clear, chewy, subtly sweet. Different from everywhere else. Reheat slightly for best texture.
- Milk popsicles from roadside vendors near the night market
- Beef noodle soup is also excellent in Hualien — local variation uses more tomato

**Tainan (if you go):**
- Dan zai noodles (擔仔麵) at Du Hsiao Yueh Restaurant (度小月) — the original, since 1895
- Coffin bread (棺材板) at Chihkan Tower vicinity
- Milkfish congee (虱目魚粥) — a Tainan morning institution
